IndiGo Baggage Policy
======================

HAND / CABIN BAGGAGE ALLOWANCE
--------------------------------
- Each passenger may carry one hand bag weighing up to 7 kg, with maximum dimensions of 115 cm (Length + Width + Height).
- In addition to the hand bag, one personal article (such as a ladies' purse or a small laptop bag) weighing no more than 3 kg is allowed.
- Maximum hand baggage dimensions: 55 cm x 35 cm x 25 cm.
- For certain international codeshare routes, cabin baggage weight limits may be 8 kg instead of 7 kg.

CHECKED-IN (CHECK-IN) BAGGAGE ALLOWANCE
------------------------------------------
- Domestic flights (within India): 15 kg free allowance per passenger. For double/multi-seat bookings, an extra 10 kg is allowed.
- International sectors vary by destination:
  - Bangkok, Bali, Kathmandu, Krabi, Male, Mauritius, Phuket: 20 kg per person.
  - Jeddah, Madinah: 30 kg per person (includes Zam Zam water allowance on flights to India).
  - Middle East, Southeast Asia, and several other international routes (e.g. Dubai, Singapore, Hong Kong, Istanbul): 30 kg per person.
  - Nairobi: 25 kg per person.
  - Jaffna: 15 kg per person.
  - India-Kuwait routes: up to 30 kg total, including any cardboard box packaging.
  - Codeshare sectors: Australia (46 kg ex-Australia / 30 kg ex-India, 2 pieces max), EU (30 kg, 2 pieces max), Africa (30 kg, 2 pieces max), US (46 kg, 2 pieces max).
- Maximum checked baggage dimensions: 158 cm (62 inches) total (Length + Width + Height). Maximum weight per piece is 23 kg.
- If domestic-to-international connections are booked on a single PNR, the international baggage allowance applies. If connecting to another airline internationally, the allowance is 15 kg, with excess charged at INR 700/kg (non-refundable for no-shows).

INFANT BAGGAGE ALLOWANCE
--------------------------
- Hand baggage: One bag up to 7 kg and 115 cm (L+W+H) per infant, both domestic and international.
- Checked baggage: NIL (no free checked baggage allowance for infants).
- One stroller or baby pram per infant is allowed free of charge.

ALCOHOL IN BAGGAGE
--------------------
- Passengers may carry up to 5 litres of alcoholic beverages in checked baggage, provided:
  - The beverage is in retail packaging, properly sealed to prevent leakage.
  - Alcohol content does not exceed 70%.
  - If alcohol content is 24% or less, the 5-litre limit does not apply.
- Alcohol purchased at the airport security hold area may be carried in cabin baggage, in a transparent resealable bag of maximum 1 litre capacity.

EXCESS BAGGAGE CHARGES
-------------------------
- Oversized items (e.g. golf bags, surfboard bags) attract excess charges:
  - Domestic: approx INR 1650 (or foreign currency equivalent).
  - International: approx INR 2500 (or foreign currency equivalent).
- Non-standard baggage charges: INR 1200 (domestic) / INR 2500 (international) for similar oversized items.
- If an item does not fit permissible limits but can be safely seated, IndiGo may allow purchase of an extra seat for that item, arranged via customer care (not available via online booking).

PROHIBITED ITEMS - CHECKED AND CABIN BAGGAGE
-----------------------------------------------
Not permitted in either checked or cabin baggage:
- Compressed gases (flammable, non-flammable, poisonous) such as butane, oxygen, liquid nitrogen, compressed gas cylinders.
- Corrosives such as acids, alkalis, mercury, wet cell batteries.
- Explosives, munitions, fireworks, flares, ammunition, handguns, pistol caps.
- Flammable liquids/solids such as lighter refills, lighter fuel, matches, paints, thinners, radioactive material.
- Oxidizing materials such as bleaching powder and peroxides.
- Poisons and infectious substances such as insecticides, weed-killers, live virus materials.
- Fish, seafood, animals, birds, insects (alive, dead, frozen, dried, or cooked).
- Items with a strong or offensive odour.
- Other dangerous articles such as magnetized materials or irritants.

PROHIBITED ITEMS - CABIN BAGGAGE ONLY
-----------------------------------------
- Dry cell batteries (loose).
- Knives, scissors, Swiss army knives, and other sharp instruments.
- Toy replicas of firearms and ammunition.
- Weapons such as whips, nunchaku, batons, or stun guns.
- Electronic devices that cannot be powered off.
- Aerosols and liquids exceeding permitted quantity (see liquid rule below).
- Any item deemed a security hazard under local law.

LIQUIDS RULE (CABIN BAGGAGE)
--------------------------------
- Liquids, gels, pastes, aerosols (e.g. beverages, shampoo, sunscreen, toothpaste) are allowed in containers of maximum 100 ml each.
- All such containers must fit inside a single transparent, resealable 1-litre plastic bag.
- Contents are subject to security screening.

RECOMMENDATIONS AND ADVISORIES
----------------------------------
- Medication, valuables, fragile, perishable, and precious items (cameras, jewellery, money, electronics) should be carried in cabin baggage, not checked baggage. IndiGo is not responsible for such items if checked in.
- Do not accept packets from unknown persons.
- Do not leave baggage unattended at any time, especially at the airport (may be treated as suspicious and removed by security).
- Declare any arms or explosive substances before baggage screening/check-in; concealment is an offence under the Aircraft Act and Rules.
- Clearly label all baggage with passenger name, address, and contact details.
- Prohibited/restricted items found during screening may be confiscated and are not returned.

BAGGAGE TRACKING AND LOST BAGGAGE
-------------------------------------
- IndiGo provides a baggage tracking service through WorldTracer for delayed or lost baggage.
- Customers are solely responsible for the carriage of their hand baggage and personal belongings.

Source: IndiGo official website (goindigo.in) - Baggage Information section, accessed 2026.
